You are starting a **Full Launch** Product Quartet session.

## What This Does

Full Launch builds on a Soft Launch that has already shipped. It expands the V1 product into a scalable, production-grade offering by adding the layers that weren't needed for initial validation.

**Prerequisites:** You should have already completed a Soft Launch (`/quartet:design` or `/quartet:critique`) and ideally shipped V1 to real users. Full Launch is most valuable when informed by real-world data.

## What Full Launch Adds

| Layer | What It Does | Why It Waits Until Now |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Safety & Compliance Review** | Dedicated review of regulatory, safety, and compliance requirements for your domain | Soft Launch validates the thesis; Full Launch ensures it scales safely |
| **Research / Feasibility Validation** | Assesses whether unproven capabilities (AI model performance, hardware constraints, etc.) can support expanded scope | V1 data reveals where feasibility assumptions held and where they didn't |
| **Go-to-Market Strategy** | Pricing, distribution, launch planning, developer relations, documentation strategy | You don't market something you haven't validated |
| **Data-Driven Iteration** | Feeds real metrics, user feedback, and learnings from V1 back into the process | You can't iterate on data you don't have yet |

## How to Use

Provide:
1. **Your original Soft Launch artifacts** (or point to the repo/session where they live)
2. **What you learned from V1** — metrics, user feedback, friction points, surprises
3. **What changed** — new constraints, expanded goals, market shifts since Soft Launch

## Process

1. Review the original Soft Launch artifacts and V1 learnings
2. Run a focused re-evaluation through the 5 core agents, informed by real data
3. Add Safety & Compliance review (domain-dependent)
4. Add Research/Feasibility validation (if applicable)
5. Add Go-to-Market strategy
6. Produce an expanded Full Launch roadmap that builds on the Soft Launch foundation

The user approves after each step. The same agent tension, challenges, and escalation rules apply.

## When to Use Full Launch

- V1 is shipped and you have real user data
- You're entering a regulated domain (healthcare, automotive, finance, AI safety)
- You're scaling from early adopters to a broader market
- Deferred items from Soft Launch have hit their activation triggers
- You need a go-to-market plan for wider distribution

## When NOT to Use Full Launch

- You haven't shipped Soft Launch yet — go back and ship first
- You're still iterating on the core thesis — use `/quartet:retro` instead
- You want to add a single feature — use `/quartet:design` for that feature
